About Me

I am a fifth year graduate student at Rutgers University. I work with Prof. Blakesley Burkhart on understanding and developing analytic models of star formation. My work is focused on comparing simulations of star-forming regions to analytic models of star formation and includes analyzing existing simulation data, running new simulations, and working on code development. You can read more about my research under the research tab or take a look at my CV here.

In addition to my research, I am dedicated to making the field of astronomy a welcoming and supportive community. I am the current President of the Rutgers Minorities in Physics and Astronomy (MiPA) group at Rutgers, I have served as a TA for multiple undergraduate courses, and I am mentoring an undergraduate researcher. You can read about my service and outreach work here and about my current and previous teaching and mentoring work here.

I grew up in Salem, Oregon and recieved an International Baccalaureate (IB) diploma from South Salem High School in 2013. I graduated with a B.A. in Physics from Reed College in May 2017 where I also took extensive coursework in English literature and danced as often as I could. I grew up around horses and I love the mountains! I have been dancing (including ballet and Irish dance) for most of my life. In my free time, I love to play the flute, bake, and knit, and I am a voracious reader, especially of science fiction and fantasy.

You can email me at appel at physics.rutgers.edu. If for some reason that is unsuccessful, you can also reach me at sabrina.m.appel at gmail.com.

You can also find me on github.

Research

My current research is focused on understanding star formation and how it is influenced by various physical processes. In particular, my work compares simulations of star-forming regions to analytic models of star formation. I am especially interested in the impact of different stellar feedback processes, such as protostellar outflows. My work includes analyzing existing simulation data, running new simulations, and developing new modules for star formation simulations, including the simulation code TORCH.

Service & Outreach

I am currently the President (and was previously the Graduate Chair) of the Rutgers Minorities in Physics and Astronomy (MiPA) group. MiPA is a group that is dedicated to furthering the interests of and developing a sense of community among members of the Rutgers Physics and Astronomy department who are underrepresented or historically marginalized in Physics and Astronomy. As a leader of MiPA, I helped to draft and implement a Charter and Code of Conduct for MiPA. I also founded our Equity and Inclusion Journal Club. Over my time with MiPA, I have led or helped organize various events and initiatives.

For more about my service and outreach roles, please take a look at my CV.

Teaching & Mentoring

I believe teaching and mentoring are incredibly important and I find working with students to be deeply rewarding. I have served as a TA for several undergraduate courses of various levels. In the fall of 2022, I was a TA for an undergraduate Computational Astrophysics course where I helped juniors and seniors progress from the basics of coding in python to running astrophysical simulations.

In addition, I have been mentoring an undergraduate student (recently graduated!!), Avery Kiihne, on a research project investigating fitting methods of simulated column density PDFs. For more about my previous teaching and tutoring experiences, please take a look at my CV.
